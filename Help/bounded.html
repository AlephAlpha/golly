Bounded grids with various topologies (plane, torus, Klein bottle, cross-surface) can be created by adding a special suffix to the usual rule string. For example, B3/S23:T80,50 creates a toroidal Life universe 80 cells wide and 50 cells high. The suffix syntax is best illustrated by these examples:

:P40,30 — plane 40 cells wide and 30 cells high
:P40,0 — plane with finite width but infinite height
:T0,30 — tube with infinite width and height 30
:T40,30 — torus with width 40 and height 30
:T40+5,30 — torus with a shift of +5 on the horizontal edges
:T40,30-2 — torus with a shift of -2 on the vertical edges
:K40*,30 — Klein bottle with the horizontal edges twisted
:K40,30* — Klein bottle with the vertical edges twisted
:K40*+1,30 — Klein bottle with a shift on the horizontal edges
:C40,30 — cross-surface (horizontal and vertical edges are twisted)

Note that if a bounded grid has width w and height h then the cell in the top left corner has coordinates -w/2,-h/2. The different topologies are described in the following sections.
